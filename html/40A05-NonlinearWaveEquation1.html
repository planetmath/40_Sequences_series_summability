Nonlinear wave equation

A general nonlinear wave equation can be expressed as

(N⁢W⁢E)⁢{ut⁢t-ux⁢x=f⁢(x,t,u) in ⁢(0,π)×ℝu⁢(0,t)=u⁢(π,t)=0∀t∈ℝ

where f⁢(x,t,s) is a continuous, monotonic increasing or decreasing (in s) real function on [0,π]×ℝ2 and 2⁢π-periodic in t. Nontrivial periodic solutions are of interest in many applications. Existence of multiple or even infinitely many periodic solutions to some special cases of (NWE), e.g., f⁢(x,t,s) is superlinear in s and/or independent of the time t, have been proved by many authors such as P. Rabinowitz, V. Benci, M. Berti, etc.
