proof that Euler’s constant exists


Theorem 1

The limit

γ=limn→∞⁡(∑k=1n1k-ln⁡n)

exists.

Proof. Let

Cn=11+12+⋯+1n-ln⁡n

and

Dn=Cn-1n

Then

Cn+1-Cn=1n+1-ln⁡(1+1n)

and

Dn+1-Dn=1n-ln⁡(1+1n)

Now, by considering the Taylor series for ln⁡(1+x), we see that

1n+1<ln⁡(1+1n)<1n

and so

Cn+1-Cn<0<Dn+1-Dn

Thus, the Cn decrease monotonically, while the Dn increase monotonically, since the differences are negative (positive for Dn). Further, Dn<Cn and thus D1=0 is a lower bound for Cn. Thus the Cn are monotonically decreasing and bounded below, so they must converge.
