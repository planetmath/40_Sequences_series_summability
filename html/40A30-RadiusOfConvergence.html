radius of convergence


To the power series

∑k=0∞ak⁢(x-x0)k (1)

there exists a number r∈[0,∞], its radius of convergence, such that the series converges absolutely for all (real or complex) numbers x with |x-x0|<r and diverges whenever |x-x0|>r. This is known as Abel’s theorem on power series. (For |x-x0|=r no general statements can be made.)

The radius of convergence is given by:

r=lim infk→∞⁡1|ak|k (2)

and can also be computed as

r=limk→∞⁡|akak+1|, (3)

if this limit exists.

It follows from the Weierstrass M-test (http://planetmath.org/WeierstrassMTest) that for any radius r′ smaller than the radius of convergence, the power series converges uniformly within the closed disk of radius r′.
